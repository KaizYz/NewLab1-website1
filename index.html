Histogram Equalization Process

The algorithm enhances image contrast by redistributing pixel intensities across the full dynamic range. The process involves computing pixel frequency distributions, calculating cumulative probabilities, and applying transformation mappings to achieve optimal brightness balance.

Mathematical Equivalence

Through rigorous mathematical analysis, we proved that applying Gaussian smoothing followed by derivative filtering is mathematically equivalent to directly applying a Derivative of Gaussian filter. This fundamental principle enables significant computational optimization without sacrificing accuracy.

Part 2E: Image Sharpening

Unsharp masking technique with variable alpha parameters for optimal detail enhancement

Unsharp Masking Technique

This sophisticated sharpening method extracts high-frequency image details by subtracting a smoothed version from the original image, then adds these enhanced details back with a controllable intensity parameter (alpha) to achieve optimal sharpness without introducing artifacts.

‚ö°

Parameter Guidelines

Conservative (1-2): Recommended for most images - Subtle enhancement without artifacts
Moderate (3-5): Good for soft or slightly blurred images
Aggressive (8-10): High enhancement but risk of introducing artifacts
